Hint 1: find c directly from V=IR on the 15 Ω branch. Then the whole bottom branch's pd (=c) equals d, the battery pd, since it's the only resistor in that branch.
Hint 2: once you have d, use Kirchhoff's voltage law on the top branch to find e (d − 8 V), then Ohm's law on the 8 Ω resistor to find f. Finally g = f + 0.8 A.
c = I×R = 0.8 × 15 = 12 V
d = pd across the whole bottom branch (just the 15 Ω resistor) = 12 V
e = d − 8 V = 12 − 8 = 4 V (Kirchhoff's voltage law on the top branch)
f = e / 8 Ω = 4/8 = 0.5 A (Ohm's law, same current through both series resistors)
g = f + 0.8 = 0.5 + 0.8 = 1.3 A (Kirchhoff's current law)
