Collaborative Project

1 resource
  • Energy Balance & the Greenhouse Effect — Student Workbook

    A two-lesson workbook on radiation, emissivity, the solar constant and albedo, followed by Earth's energy balance and the greenhouse effect. Includes a synthesis task on real planetary energy budgets, an interactive inverse-square-law simulation, and the brief for the 10-hour Collaborative Sciences Project investigating albedo or foliage energy shielding, with an understanding checklist and self-marking answer key.

Currents and Circuits

10 resources
  • Modelling Electrical Circuits — Self-Marking Workbook

    Builds intuition for circuit behaviour using everyday analogies (a coal truck, a supermarket, and a hamster wheel) before mapping each model onto real electric circuit quantities. Self-marking format for independent study.

  • Potential Difference and Kirchhoff's Laws — Interactive Notes

    Introduces potential difference via the gravitational analogy, then develops Kirchhoff's voltage law and current law, with interactive checks and a results summary.

  • Basic Circuits — IB Physics Workbook

    Practical workbook covering the definition of current, the charge equation I = ΔQ/Δt, and hands-on measurement of current and potential difference in series and parallel circuits, finishing with a summary of Kirchhoff's laws.

  • DC Circuit Builder

    Interactive tool for constructing DC circuits from a palette of components, with the ability to save and load circuit designs — useful for experimenting with circuit layouts before building them physically.

  • Charge, Current and Time — Practice Workbook

    Practice problems on the relationship between charge, current and time, covering unit conversions, multi-step problems, and electron counting, with an extension section for further challenge.

  • Basic Circuits Concept Check

    Ten circuit diagrams testing conceptual understanding of series and parallel circuit behaviour — a quick diagnostic to check readiness before moving on to resistance.

  • Resistance

    Notes on what resistance is and how it depends on temperature via the Drude model, followed by a practical on measuring resistance across six different circuits.

  • Adding Resistors — Self-Marking Workbook

    Covers combining resistors in series and parallel using rope-bridge and water-pump analogies, then a workbook, a predicted-vs-measured practical, and a concept check on resistor circuits.

  • Potentiometer Simulation

    Interactive simulation exploring variable resistor and potentiometer control, letting students see how sliding a wiper changes potential difference and current in a circuit.

  • EMF and Internal Resistance — Self-Marking Workbook

    Notes on emf, terminal potential difference, "lost volts", and internal resistance, including finding emf and r from a graph, an interactive simulation, worked examples, and a review workbook.

Electromagnetism

4 resources
  • Electric and Magnetic Fields — Student Workbook

    Covers electric charge, charging and discharging by induction, Coulomb's law (with an interactive inverse-square-law simulation), and electric fields.

  • Millikan's Oil-Drop Experiment — Student Workbook

    An interactive workbook and simulation recreating Millikan's oil-drop experiment: balancing the forces on a charged drop, collecting simulated data, deducing the elementary charge from repeated measurements, and evaluating the experiment's assumptions and sources of uncertainty.

  • Motion in Electromagnetic Fields — Student Workbook

    Covers conventional current versus the flow of electrons, and the motion of charged particles in uniform electric and uniform magnetic fields, finishing with a "predict the path" quiz.

  • Electromagnetic Induction — Student Workbook

    Covers motional emf, magnetic flux and flux linkage, Faraday's law and Lenz's law, AC generators and the effect of frequency, and transformers (including a 3D transformer thermal simulation), with an equation summary and glossary.

Tools

4 resources
  • Accuracy and Precision — IB Physics Practice

    Self-marking practice distinguishing accuracy (closeness to the true value, affected by systematic error) from precision (repeatability, affected by random error and instrument resolution), using the classic dartboard illustration.

  • Scientific Notation (Standard Form) — IB Physics Practice

    Self-marking practice writing numbers in and out of scientific notation, calculating with standard form, and applying it to real IB Physics problems from Topic 1: Measurements and uncertainties. Includes a foundational task and an extension task with hints.

  • Significant Figures & Estimates — IB Physics Practice

    Self-marking practice on identifying and rounding to significant figures and carrying them through calculations, followed by a section on order-of-magnitude estimation for everyday quantities.

  • A-Level Uncertainty

    A saved reference page (via Dr Jones Physics) on calculating and combining measurement uncertainties, covering absolute, fractional and percentage uncertainty and how they propagate through calculations.
